Overview

Outlast is a first-person survival horror video game, developed and published by Red Barrels. Released in 2013, the game is set in the recently abandoned Mount Massive Asylum for the Criminally Insane. You play as investigative journalist Miles Upshur as he explores the asylum to uncover the truth. The DLC Outlast: WhistleBlower was released in 2014 and offers a different perspective of the same night from the eyes of software enginner Waylon Park.

Gameplay

Outlast is known for its tense atmosphere and lack of combat mechanics, mimicking the helplessness of the characters. Players must rely on the iconic camcorder night vision mechanic of the game in order to progress.

Story

The game follows investigative journalist Miles Upshur, who breaks into the Mount Massive Asylum after receiving an anonymous tip from a whistleblower inside Mount Massive. Once inside, he discovers evidence of horrific experiments and encounters dangerous inmates. A fanatic patient dressed in a priest costume forces Miles to chase him around the asylum, giving him footage of the crimes being commited and of the mysterious entity known as the Walrider.

Reviews

Outlast received positive reviews for its atmosphere and intense gameplay. Critics praised its ability to evoke fear and tension without relying on traditional combat mechanics. It was praised for being so advanced for its time, and continues to be one of the most iconic horror franchises.

"Outlast is one of the scariest games ever made." – IGN
"A nightmarish experience from start to finish." – GameSpot

Outlast Characters

Waylon Park

Waylon Park is the protagonist of the Outlast: Whistleblower DLC. He is a software engineer who sends the initial email to Miles Upshur and ultimately escapes to expos the horrors at Mount Massive Asylum.

Chris Walker

Chris Walker is a former veteran and security officer at Mount Massive Asylum who has become one of the most dangerous Variants. He's known for his imposing size and brutal strength, as well as calling Miles Upshur "little pig."

Miles Upshur

Miles Upshur is the main protagonist of the original Outlast game. He is an investigative journalist who discovers the dark secrets of Mount Massive Asylum.

Jeremy Blaire

Jeremy Blaire is the antagonist of the Whistleblower DLC. He is a high-ranking executive at the Murkoff Corporation, the business funding Mount Massive Asylum and Project Walrider.

Richard Trager

Richard Trager is one of the Variants and a sadistic surgeon at Mount Massive Asylum who performs cruel experiments on the patients. He was formly an employee at Mount Massive and friends with Jeremy Blaire. However, an incident in which he stabbed a pregnant woman with scissors caused him to be forcefully admitted in order to cover the scandal.

Billy Hope

Billy Hope is a patient at Mount Massive Asylum and the host of the Walrider, a supernatural entity controlling many of the asylum’s events.

Rudolf Wernicke

Dr. Rudolf Wernicke is the scientist behind the Walrider project at Mount Massive Asylum. His unethical research led to the creation of the Variants and the Walrider.

Eddie Gluskin

Eddie Gluskin, also known as "The Groom," is a terrifying Variant who kidnaps male inmates in an attempt to turn them into his "brides." He is the main obstacle Waylon encounters in Whistleblower.

Frank Manera

Frank Manera is a cannibalistic Variant that players encounter in the Whistleblower DLC. In the game, the player encounters him in the kitchen, where he has strung up bodies and is microwaving a head when Waylon finds him. He chases Waylon around with a saw, and shoves him into a brick oven that Waylon ultimately escapes from.

Father Martin

Father Martin is the patient that guides Miles through the asylum. He has delusions that Miles is the apostle sent by God to spread the word of the Walrider.

Variants

The Variants are the grotesque and dangerous former inmates of Mount Massive Asylum, each transformed by the unethical experiments conducted by Murkoff Corporation. Throughout the game, the player encounters both passive and aggrsive variants.
